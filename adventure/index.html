Arriving at the Grand Canyon

Day 1

I started the day at home in my bed, and I ended the day at a hotel overlooks the Grand Canyon. I went in a limo to the airport so I could look for the secret treasure in the Grand Canyon. I got to my hotel, which was named "Grand Canyon Village", around 5:00 PM and here I am now. This is a list of important stuff I brought:

  • Water
  • Food
  • Treasure Clues
  • Sunscreen
  • Swimsuit

The treasure clues were found in the envelope sent to me. It's been a crazy day, but I only know one thing. I am looking for treasure. They call this the "Gateway to wonder", so it must it's a gateway to the treasure.

Looking for Clues

Day 2

I woke up today with a mission. The first clue said, "The noise that monkeys make will lead you on." I did some research and it turns out there is an "Ooh Aah" trail in the Canyon. On my way there I crossed a skywalk called "The Grand Canyon Skywalk". I strongly believe in the saying, "Always take the scenic route," which means always go the way that will give you the best views. I hiked into the Canyon and I got to Ooh Aah point. One the back of the sign it said, "Water throughout the fall will cause you to find what you are looking for." I realized that the treasure must be behind the waterfall. It was getting late, so I had to go the next day.


Treasure

Day 3

The next day I went down to the bottom of the canyon on the Bright Angel Trail. There was a warning sign saying, "Use caution near the edge," which was a warning to make sure you don't fall in the Canyon. I borrowed a raft from the Colorado River Rafting building at the bottom of the Canyon. I went to the main waterfall I knew, Havasu Falls. I knew there must be a secret room behind it, so I went straigth through it. I was soaked and spitting water everywhere, but I saw the room where the treasure was. I opened the secret door and there it was.... The Treasure was right in front of me. There were countless items of jewelry, gold, and old historic Native American treasure. I took it all and I am planning to donate it all to a museum.
